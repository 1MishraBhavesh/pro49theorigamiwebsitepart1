Interesting Facts about camel

  1. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms
  2. Mother camels carry their calves up to 14 months before giving birth.
  3. When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go

Interesting Facts about pigeon

  1. Pigeons have been known to live over 30 years
  2. A full grown pigeon has 10,000 feathers.
  3. Pigeons can navigate via the earth’s magnetic fields

Interesting Facts about Flying cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  2. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  3. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.

Interesting Facts about chameleon

  1. Their feet work like salad tongs.
  2. chameleons vary wildly in terms of size.
  3. Skin crystals enable them to change color at will.

Interesting Facts about panda

  1. A panda Can Poop 28 Kilos per day.
  2. You can see panda babies in August.
  3. Pandas have 6 toes to grasp bamboo.

Interesting Facts about teddy bear

  1. The Teddy Bears' Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step.
  2. The story of Goldilocks And The Three Bears was written in 1837 by Robert Southey who was the Poet Laureate at the time.
  3. In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.
